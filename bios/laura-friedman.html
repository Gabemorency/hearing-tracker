Laura Friedman
United States House of Representatives
District 30 · California · Democrat
Biography

Laura Friedman was born and raised in California and built her early career as a film and television producer in the entertainment industry before transitioning to public service. She earned her education in the United States and went on to serve in the California State Assembly, representing the 43rd District starting in 2016, where she developed a reputation as a pragmatic legislator focused on transportation, environmental policy, and land use reform.

Friedman was elected to represent California's 30th Congressional District in the 2024 election, succeeding Adam Schiff who vacated the seat to pursue a successful U.S. Senate campaign. She brought to Congress a legislative record that included authoring California's landmark "safe streets" legislation aimed at reducing pedestrian and cyclist fatalities, as well as significant work on climate and housing policy during her years in the state legislature. She is widely recognized as a leading voice on sustainable transportation and urban planning issues.

In her current role in the U.S. House of Representatives, Friedman continues to prioritize transportation safety, climate resilience, and housing affordability as central pillars of her legislative agenda. She has sought committee assignments that align with her expertise in infrastructure and environmental issues, positioning herself as an advocate for smart growth and pedestrian-friendly urban policy at the federal level. Friedman remains a prominent figure among California Democrats focused on connecting local land use decisions to broader national climate and transportation goals.
