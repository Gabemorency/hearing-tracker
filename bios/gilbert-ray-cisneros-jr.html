Biography

Gilbert Ray Cisneros, Jr. was born and raised in Monterey Park, California, and is of Mexican-American heritage. He earned a bachelor's degree from Georgetown University and later received a Master of Arts in Organizational Management from the University of La Verne, and he served as a Naval officer for a decade before winning a $266 million Mega Millions lottery jackpot in 2010, which he used to fund philanthropic initiatives focused on education and veterans' services. Prior to his congressional career, Cisneros founded the Gilbert and Jacki Cisneros Foundation, dedicating significant resources to college scholarship programs for Latino students and support networks for military families.

Cisneros was first elected to represent California's 39th Congressional District in 2018, flipping a traditionally Republican seat in a closely contested race. During his time in Congress, he served on the House Armed Services Committee and the House Veterans' Affairs Committee, leveraging his military background to advocate for veterans' benefits, military readiness, and education funding. He became particularly known for championing scholarship access for underserved communities and pushing for policies that expanded support for service members and their families.

Following redistricting, Cisneros ran for and won election in California's 31st Congressional District, continuing to represent constituents in the San Gabriel Valley region. He remains focused on issues including affordable education, veterans' care, economic opportunity, and community investment in his diverse district. His recent legislative priorities have centered on expanding access to higher education, supporting small businesses, and ensuring robust federal resources for the military communities that shape his district.
