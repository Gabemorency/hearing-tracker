Biography

Jennifer A. Kiggans was born and raised in Virginia and went on to build a distinguished career in both military service and medicine. She earned her medical degree and trained as a nurse practitioner, specializing in geriatrics, while also serving as a naval aviator in the United States Navy, where she flew the SH-60 Seahawk helicopter during her active duty service. Before entering federal politics, Kiggans served in the Virginia State Senate, representing the 7th district and establishing herself as a voice on veterans' and healthcare issues.

Kiggans was first elected to the United States House of Representatives in 2022, defeating incumbent Democrat Elaine Luria in Virginia's 2nd Congressional District, which encompasses the Hampton Roads region and Virginia Beach. She serves on the House Veterans' Affairs Committee and the House Armed Services Committee, assignments that reflect both her personal military background and the district's large population of active-duty service members and veterans. She is best known for her strong advocacy on behalf of the military community and her focus on national defense and healthcare access for veterans.

In her current role, Kiggans continues to represent one of the most defense-heavy districts in the nation, working to support the military installations and personnel that are central to the Hampton Roads economy and community. She has focused on legislative priorities including military readiness, veterans' benefits, and coastal resilience issues relevant to the Tidewater region. Kiggans remains an active voice within the House Republican Conference on matters of national security and the welfare of the men and women who serve in the armed forces.
