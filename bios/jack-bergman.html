Jack Bergman

District 1 · Michigan · Republican
Subcmte. Chair
Biography

Jack Bergman was born on February 2, 1947, and raised in Shakopee, Minnesota, where he developed a strong work ethic rooted in Midwestern values. He earned a bachelor's degree from Gustavus Adolphus College and later received a master's degree in business administration from the University of West Florida. Before entering politics, Bergman enjoyed a distinguished military career spanning over three decades, retiring as a Lieutenant General in the United States Marine Corps — one of the highest-ranking Marine officers to serve in the Corps' history.

Bergman was first elected to Congress in 2016, representing Michigan's 1st Congressional District, which encompasses the entire Upper Peninsula and a significant portion of the northern Lower Peninsula. He has served on the House Veterans' Affairs Committee and the House Budget Committee, bringing his military background to bear on issues of national defense, fiscal responsibility, and veterans' care. He is widely recognized as a strong advocate for the military community and for the rural constituents of northern Michigan, championing issues related to natural resources, agriculture, and economic development in his expansive district.

In his current role, Bergman continues to represent one of the largest congressional districts east of the Mississippi River, focusing on policies that support veterans, strengthen national security, and promote the economic interests of rural Michigan. He has been a consistent voice for responsible federal spending and has worked to protect the Great Lakes region's natural resources and support local industries such as timber, mining, and tourism. Bergman remains actively engaged in efforts to expand rural broadband access and improve healthcare infrastructure for the underserved communities throughout his district.
