Claudia Tenney
United States House of Representatives
District 24 · New York · Republican
Biography

Claudia Tenney was born and raised in New Hartford, New York, and comes from a family with deep roots in journalism and public service, as her father was a state assemblyman. She earned her undergraduate degree from Colgate University and her law degree from the Taft School of Law at the University of Cincinnati, going on to work as an attorney and also serving as a co-owner and operator of Mid-York Press, a family newspaper publishing business. Before her congressional career, Tenney served in the New York State Assembly from 2011 to 2017, where she built a reputation as a fiscally conservative voice in state politics.

Tenney was first elected to the U.S. House of Representatives in 2016, representing New York's 22nd Congressional District, before losing her seat in 2018 and winning a narrow victory in 2020 for the same district. She has served on the House Financial Services Committee and the Foreign Affairs Committee, where she has been a vocal advocate for Israel, Taiwan, and a strong U.S. foreign policy posture. Tenney is perhaps best known for her staunch support of the Second Amendment, her efforts to reduce federal regulations on small businesses, and her close alignment with former President Donald Trump.

Following redistricting, Tenney now represents New York's 24th Congressional District and continues to serve on committees addressing financial and international affairs. She remains a prominent voice on issues including tax policy, election integrity, and countering Chinese influence abroad, frequently advocating for stronger U.S.-Taiwan relations. Tenney has also focused on constituent services and economic development for her upstate New York district, championing manufacturing, agriculture, and small business interests in the region.
