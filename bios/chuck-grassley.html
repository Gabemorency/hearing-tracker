Chuck Grassley
United States Senate
Iowa · Republican
President Pro Tempore
Biography

Charles Ernest Grassley was born on September 17, 1933, in New Hartford, Butler County, Iowa, where he was raised on a family farm that he continues to work to this day. He earned his bachelor's and master's degrees in political science from the University of Northern Iowa and pursued doctoral work at the University of Iowa, before beginning a career combining farming with public service. His mother was among the first Iowa women to vote after ratification of the Nineteenth Amendment, and civic engagement was instilled in him from an early age.

Grassley was first elected to the Iowa House of Representatives in 1958 at age 25, serving there until 1974 when he won election to the U.S. House of Representatives, and then in 1980 he defeated incumbent Democratic Senator John Culver to begin his Senate career. He has been reelected seven times since, making him the longest-serving senator in Iowa history, the longest-serving Republican in congressional history, and the sixth-longest-serving U.S. senator of all time. During his Senate tenure he has chaired the Finance, Judiciary, and Special Committee on Aging committees, and built a reputation as one of Congress's foremost champions of whistleblower protections and government oversight.

Grassley currently serves as President Pro Tempore of the United States Senate, making him third in the presidential line of succession. At 92 years old he is the oldest sitting U.S. senator and holds the longest consecutive voting record in Senate history. He continues his annual tradition of holding public meetings in all 99 of Iowa's counties each year.
