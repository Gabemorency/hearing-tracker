Ilhan Omar
United States House of Representatives
District 5 · Minnesota · Democrat
Subcmte. Ranking Member
Biography

Ilhan Omar was born in Mogadishu, Somalia, and spent several years in a refugee camp in Kenya before immigrating to the United States with her family at the age of twelve, eventually settling in Minneapolis, Minnesota. She earned a Bachelor of Science degree in Political Science and International Studies from North Dakota State University and went on to work as a community educator and later as a political organizer before pursuing elected office. Prior to her congressional career, Omar served in the Minnesota House of Representatives beginning in 2017, becoming the first Somali American and one of the first Muslim women elected to a state legislature in the United States.

Omar was first elected to the U.S. House of Representatives in 2018, representing Minnesota's 5th Congressional District, and was one of the first two Muslim women elected to Congress. She has served on the House Foreign Affairs Committee and the House Education and Labor Committee, where she has worked on issues related to international human rights, labor rights, and economic equality. Omar is widely known as a member of "The Squad," a group of progressive Democratic congresswomen, and has been a prominent voice on issues including immigration reform, Medicare for All, and foreign policy.

Omar currently serves as a member of the House Foreign Affairs Committee and remains one of the most visible progressive voices in the Democratic caucus. She continues to advocate for policies addressing economic inequality, affordable housing, student debt relief, and comprehensive immigration reform. Omar has also been a consistent critic of U.S. foreign policy in the Middle East and has pushed for greater accountability and humanitarian considerations in American international engagement.
