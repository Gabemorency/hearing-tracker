Jasmine Crockett
United States House of Representatives
District 30 · Texas · Democrat
Subcmte. Ranking Member
Biography

Jasmine Crockett was born and raised in Dallas, Texas, and pursued her higher education at Rhodes College in Memphis, Tennessee, before earning her law degree from the University of Houston Law Center. Crockett built her professional career as a civil rights and criminal defense attorney, advocating for marginalized communities and working on issues related to social justice and voting rights before transitioning into public service.

Crockett was first elected to the Texas House of Representatives in 2020, where she quickly gained recognition for her outspoken advocacy on voting rights and criminal justice reform, before winning election to the U.S. House of Representatives in 2022 to represent Texas's 30th Congressional District. She serves on the House Oversight and Accountability Committee as well as the House Agriculture Committee, and has become one of the more visible and vocal Democratic members of Congress, known for her sharp questioning during high-profile committee hearings. Crockett is widely recognized for her willingness to directly challenge political opponents and for her passionate defense of democratic principles and civil liberties.

In her current role, Crockett continues to serve as a prominent progressive voice within the Democratic caucus, focusing on issues including voting rights, reproductive rights, economic justice, and police accountability. She has been an active participant in national conversations around democratic norms and governmental oversight, frequently appearing in media to articulate the Democratic perspective on pressing legislative matters. Crockett remains committed to representing the diverse constituents of the Dallas-area district while working to advance policies that address systemic inequality at the federal level.
