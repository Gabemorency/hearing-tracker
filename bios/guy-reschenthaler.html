Guy Reschenthaler
United States House of Representatives
District 14 · Pennsylvania · Republican
Biography

Guy Reschenthaler was born and raised in southwestern Pennsylvania, growing up in the Pittsburgh area before pursuing his education at Duquesne University School of Law, where he earned his Juris Doctor. Before entering elected office, Reschenthaler served as a Lieutenant Commander in the United States Navy Judge Advocate General's Corps, including a deployment to Iraq, and later worked as a criminal defense attorney and district court judge in Allegheny County, Pennsylvania.

Reschenthaler was first elected to the United States House of Representatives in 2018, representing Pennsylvania's 14th Congressional District, which encompasses counties in the southwestern corner of the state. He has served on the House Judiciary Committee and the House Rules Committee, where he has been a vocal advocate for law enforcement, veterans' issues, and conservative judicial principles. He is perhaps best known for his outspoken support of former President Donald Trump and his work promoting energy development and economic growth in his western Pennsylvania district.

Reschenthaler currently serves in House Republican leadership, having been elected to the position of Republican Chief Deputy Whip, making him one of the higher-ranking members of the House GOP leadership structure. He has focused heavily on energy policy, particularly championing natural gas and fossil fuel development critical to his region's economy, as well as border security and fiscal conservatism. Reschenthaler has also remained active on national security matters, drawing on his military background to inform his legislative work and public commentary.
